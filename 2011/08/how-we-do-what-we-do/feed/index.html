]]> By: Stina https://orangette.net/2011/08/how-we-do-what-we-do/#comment-29822 Fri, 07 Oct 2011 19:11:56 +0000 https://elitemporaryblog.wordpress.com/2011/08/04/how-we-do-what-we-do#comment-29822 Thank you for your writing. I've come to realize that it's a warning sign that I'm afraid of confronting something in that cave whenever I start avoiding writing purposefully. I'm a writer and it's how I think…even if I find myself not writing regularly.

It doesn't seem anyone's mentioned Twyla tharp's book The Creative Habit. If sitting down to write is harder than the actual writing she argues training your body to have a routine shortcuts the ability to self doubt and procrastinate. Maybe those writers who “wake up and write every morning” don't find it any easier– it's just what they've done for so long, they can't NOT sit down to that paper each morning.

But I also agree with others who talk about the “writing” that happens in your head as you're processing and creating internally before the itch gets you to sit down and pour it out.
